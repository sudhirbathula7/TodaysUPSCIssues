ISSUE TITLE
India's Semiconductor Ecosystem and Strategic Chip Manufacturing

GS PAPER
GS III

SUBJECT
Science & Technology | Economy | Industrial Policy

RATING
5.0/5

RECALL QUESTION 1
Why are semiconductors considered strategic assets in the modern global economy?

RECALL QUESTION 2
Why is India focusing on mature-node chip manufacturing before advanced semiconductor fabrication?

CURRENT CONTEXT
The Union Cabinet has approved the second phase of the India Semiconductor Mission with a ₹1.27 lakh crore outlay to strengthen semiconductor design, manufacturing, research and the supporting electronics ecosystem.

WHY IT MATTERS FOR UPSC
Semiconductors influence technological self-reliance, national security, industrial competitiveness, global supply chains and India's ambition to emerge as a trusted electronics manufacturing hub.

CORE CONCEPT
A semiconductor ecosystem includes chip design, fabrication, packaging, materials, equipment, research, intellectual property, skilled manpower and resilient manufacturing infrastructure.

CHALLENGES
High capital costs, technology dependence, infrastructure gaps, skilled workforce shortages, supply-chain vulnerabilities and rapid technological advancements remain major constraints.

WAY FORWARD
Strengthen industry-academia collaboration, expand semiconductor research, build reliable infrastructure, develop skilled talent, encourage domestic innovation and deepen integration with global value chains.

QUICK FACTS
• India Semiconductor Mission Phase-II Outlay: ₹1.27 lakh crore.
• Mature-node chips (28 nm and above) account for nearly 70% of global demand.
• Semiconductor design receives subsidies of up to 75% under the new policy.
• India aims to gradually develop advanced 7 nm and 2 nm chip capabilities.

WHAT UPSC ASKS
Why is semiconductor self-reliance strategically important for India? Discuss the opportunities and challenges in developing a domestic semiconductor ecosystem.

KEY TAKEAWAY
A strong semiconductor ecosystem is essential for India's technological sovereignty, industrial growth and long-term strategic resilience.

Climate Change, Heat Stress and Sleep as a Public Health Challenge

GS PAPER
GS III | GS II

SUBJECT

RATING
5.0/5

RECALL QUESTION 1
How does climate change affect human sleep and overall public health?

RECALL QUESTION 2
Why should sleep health become an integral part of urban and public health planning?

CURRENT CONTEXT
Studies indicate that rising night-time temperatures linked to climate change are reducing sleeping hours across Indian cities, highlighting sleep deprivation as an emerging public health concern.

WHY IT MATTERS FOR UPSC
The issue links climate change, urbanisation and public health while influencing productivity, healthcare costs, non-communicable diseases and climate adaptation policies.

CORE CONCEPT
Sleep is a vital biological process supporting immunity, metabolism, cognitive function and cellular repair, making healthy sleep an important component of preventive healthcare.

CHALLENGES
Warmer nights, urban heat islands, poor housing, lifestyle changes, excessive screen exposure and inadequate public awareness contribute to chronic sleep deprivation.

WAY FORWARD
Integrate sleep health into public health programmes, strengthen heat action plans, improve urban planning, promote affordable cooling solutions and encourage healthy lifestyle practices.

QUICK FACTS
• Rising night-time temperatures are linked to significant annual sleep loss.
• Urban heat island effects worsen heat stress in densely populated cities.
• Chronic sleep deprivation increases the risk of hypertension, diabetes and cardiovascular diseases.
• Heat Action Plans primarily address daytime heat but increasingly require night-time interventions.

WHAT UPSC ASKS
Discuss how climate change is emerging as a public health challenge with special reference to sleep deprivation and urban heat stress.

KEY TAKEAWAY
Protecting healthy sleep requires coordinated action across climate policy, urban planning and preventive public health systems.

Combating Synthetic Drug Networks and Strengthening India's Anti-Narcotics Framework

GS PAPER
GS III | GS II

SUBJECT

RATING
4.8/5

RECALL QUESTION 1
Why have synthetic drugs become a major internal security and public health concern in India?

RECALL QUESTION 2
How can technology strengthen anti-narcotics enforcement and prosecution?

CURRENT CONTEXT
Kerala has intensified anti-drug enforcement through Operation Toofan, combining inter-state coordination, intelligence sharing, rehabilitation and stronger investigation against synthetic drug networks.

WHY IT MATTERS FOR UPSC
Drug trafficking affects internal security, organised crime, youth welfare, cybercrime, public health and cooperative federalism in law enforcement.

CORE CONCEPT
An effective anti-narcotics framework combines prevention, enforcement, intelligence sharing, cyber forensics, rehabilitation and coordinated prosecution against organised criminal networks.

CHALLENGES
Digital drug networks, encrypted communication, weak forensic evidence, inter-state trafficking, limited cyber capabilities and complex criminal syndicates hinder effective enforcement.

WAY FORWARD
Strengthen cyber forensic capacity, enhance intelligence sharing, improve inter-state coordination, modernise investigative agencies, expand rehabilitation services and ensure speedy prosecution.

QUICK FACTS
• Operation Toofan is Kerala's integrated anti-drug enforcement initiative.
• NCORD promotes national coordination against narcotics trafficking.
• NIDAAN maintains a database of arrested narcotics offenders.
• Synthetic drugs increasingly dominate organised narcotics trafficking networks.

WHAT UPSC ASKS
Examine the growing challenge of synthetic drug trafficking in India and evaluate measures needed to strengthen the country's anti-narcotics framework.

KEY TAKEAWAY
Combating narcotics requires integrated enforcement, technological capability, community participation and coordinated action across governments and agencies.
