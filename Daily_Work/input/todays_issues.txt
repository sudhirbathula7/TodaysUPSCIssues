ISSUE: Strategic Reserves for Economic Security

CURRENT CONTEXT:
Recent geopolitical conflicts have highlighted how adequate reserves of oil, food and foreign exchange can stabilise prices and protect economies from climate, supply chain and geopolitical disruptions.

WHY IT MATTERS FOR UPSC:
Strategic reserves strengthen economic resilience, food security, energy security, inflation management and macroeconomic stability, making them important for governance, disaster preparedness and national security.

CORE CONCEPT:
Strategic reserves are government-maintained emergency stocks of critical commodities and financial assets used to ensure uninterrupted supply and economic stability during crises.

CHALLENGES:
High storage costs, wastage risks, fiscal burden, inventory management, infrastructure gaps and balancing reserve levels with market efficiency remain significant policy challenges.

WAY FORWARD:
Develop an integrated strategic reserve policy, modernise storage infrastructure, improve stock monitoring, diversify reserves, strengthen climate preparedness and periodically review reserve adequacy.

QUICK FACTS:
India maintains strategic petroleum reserves for emergency energy security.
Food Corporation of India manages buffer stocks of rice and wheat.
Foreign exchange reserves help manage external sector shocks.
Strategic reserves reduce inflationary pressures during supply disruptions.

WHAT UPSC ASKS:
Role of strategic reserves in ensuring economic resilience, food security, energy security and managing climate and geopolitical risks.

KEY TAKEAWAY:
Strong strategic reserves act as economic insurance, enabling governments to absorb crises while protecting growth, prices and national security.

ISSUE: Parliamentary Accountability and Democratic Oversight

CURRENT CONTEXT:
The Monsoon Session begins amid major political realignments, making parliamentary debate and legislative scrutiny crucial despite a weakened Opposition.

WHY IT MATTERS FOR UPSC:
Parliamentary accountability is essential for responsible governance, executive oversight, democratic representation and effective functioning of India's constitutional democracy.

CORE CONCEPT:
Parliament ensures democratic accountability through legislation, debates, questions, committee scrutiny, financial control and representation of public concerns.

CHALLENGES:
Weak Opposition, political defections, declining deliberative standards, disruptions and excessive executive dominance can reduce Parliament's effectiveness in democratic oversight.

WAY FORWARD:
Strengthen parliamentary committees, promote informed debates, ensure greater accountability, uphold constitutional conventions and encourage constructive bipartisan participation.

QUICK FACTS:
Article 75 establishes collective responsibility of the Council of Ministers to the Lok Sabha.
Question Hour enables MPs to seek accountability from the government.
Parliamentary Committees examine Bills and government functioning in detail.
Parliament exercises financial control through Budget approval and scrutiny.

WHAT UPSC ASKS:
Importance of Parliament in ensuring executive accountability, legislative scrutiny and strengthening democratic governance.

KEY TAKEAWAY:
A vibrant Parliament safeguards democracy by ensuring that public concerns are debated and executive power remains constitutionally accountable.

ISSUE: India-UK Comprehensive Economic and Trade Agreement (CETA)

CURRENT CONTEXT:
India and the United Kingdom have concluded CETA to expand market access, promote exports and deepen economic integration while safeguarding sensitive domestic sectors.

WHY IT MATTERS FOR UPSC:
The agreement affects trade policy, MSMEs, manufacturing competitiveness, global value chains and India's broader strategy of economic integration.

CORE CONCEPT:
A Comprehensive Economic and Trade Agreement reduces trade barriers while covering goods, services, investment, intellectual property and regulatory cooperation.

CHALLENGES:
High compliance costs, non-tariff barriers, MSME capacity constraints, sustainability standards and limited utilisation of trade agreements may restrict expected gains.

WAY FORWARD:
Improve export competitiveness, simplify compliance, strengthen quality infrastructure, support MSMEs, enhance trade awareness and build resilient industrial ecosystems.

QUICK FACTS:
The agreement provides zero-duty access for nearly 99% of India's exports to the U.K.
The U.K. is a high-income market where India enjoys a merchandise trade surplus.
Free Trade Agreements also address services, investment and regulatory cooperation.
MSMEs often face greater challenges in meeting non-tariff standards.

WHAT UPSC ASKS:
Evaluate India's Free Trade Agreements and their implications for exports, MSMEs and integration into global value chains.

KEY TAKEAWAY:
Trade agreements create opportunities only when domestic competitiveness and institutional capacity convert market access into sustained market share.

ISSUE: Private Sector Participation in India's Space Economy

CURRENT CONTEXT:
Skyroot Aerospace successfully launched the Vikram-1 rocket, marking India's first private orbital launch and signalling growing commercial participation in the space sector.

WHY IT MATTERS FOR UPSC:
Private participation supports innovation, economic growth, technological advancement, space commercialisation and India's ambition to become a leading global space economy.

CORE CONCEPT:
Space sector reforms encourage private enterprises to develop launch vehicles, satellites and space services while ISRO focuses on research and strategic missions.

CHALLENGES:
Commercial viability, global competition, regulatory uncertainty, absence of comprehensive space legislation, scaling production and maintaining launch reliability remain key concerns.

WAY FORWARD:
Enact a Space Activities Act, strengthen regulatory certainty, encourage investment, promote innovation, develop supporting infrastructure and expand global commercial partnerships.

QUICK FACTS:
The Indian Space Policy, 2023 expanded private sector participation.
Up to 49% FDI in launch vehicles is permitted through the automatic route.
ISRO primarily focuses on scientific research and strategic space missions.
India aims to expand its space economy to about $44 billion by 2033.

WHAT UPSC ASKS:
Discuss the role of private sector participation in strengthening India's space economy and technological capabilities.

KEY TAKEAWAY:
India's space reforms will succeed when technological achievements are matched by commercially sustainable and globally competitive private enterprises.
